Catching up: How does your income compare?

April 5, 2017

I was playing around with the World Bank's Povcal data, which is a study that estimates the level of income for different sectors of the population. The World Bank uses Povcal to calculate extreme poverty, but the whole dataset is quite rich and interesting to explore. While the researchers warn against alternative uses of their data other than its main purpose of estimating poverty, I though I'd plug the data into Tableau and examine. I came up with a visualization wherein I could compare two countries' level of income. To enhance this idea, I granularized the analysis and allowed the user to select the income deciles to compare. Adding the decile dimension brings a lot of new insights from the data.

I would encourage you to play around with the data. Benchmark your country with others. For instance, I can make the observation that the top 10% of the Philippine population is comparable to Canada's middle-class. Or China's richest 10% is indeed on a rise, and has recently passed the income level of Americans in the lowest 30%. There is more to explore, hope you enjoy the viz!
