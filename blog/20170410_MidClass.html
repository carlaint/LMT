Is the Power Shifting to the World's Middle Class?

April 10, 2017

 

For a lot of us macroeconomics researchers, the message of China's emerging dominance is loud and clear. It cannot be emphasized enough, and it almost seems like it is being overplayed like a broken record.

To my surprise however, those outside the realm of macro and research think that the China talk is overrated and exaggerated. The common folk can always read and hear the headlines, but do not really go beyond the soundbyte.

Hence, here is a simple data visualization that explains China's emerging share of global income. Add to that is the observation that countries in the middle-income group seem to be creeping up in their share of total wealth. (Technically, there is a difference between wealth and income but for purposes of this simplistic explanation, I will use them interchangeably.)

As you can see, China's GDP today is almost as much as the total GDP of other advanced economies combined, a huge gain from 1990.

Looking at emerging markets combined (China + others),

the contribution of middle-income economies to global wealth is at 40%.

If you are one the those believers of the cliche, "Whoever has the money has the power," then more attention should be given to the middle class.
