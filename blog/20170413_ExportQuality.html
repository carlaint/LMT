Export Goods Quality Ladder

April 13, 2017

Here is a preview of a new, updated dataset from the IMF Research Department on Export Quality.

The premise is to visualize how one country's exports compares with its peers, in a sort of ranking, which we call the "quality ladder."

There are three steps to view the viz.

  1. Select the classification: There are three types of export classifications: ACM, BEC, and SITC. All of these are generally the same, but vary only in the grouping. As an analogy, if export goods were colors, ACM would be a grouping of complementary colors, while BEC could be a grouping by analagous colors, while SITC could be a grouping of monochromatic colors. In any case, they are all colors (export goods), just with different classifications.
  2. Select the Industry: Depending on what you select in step 1 above (ACM, BEC, SITC), you are given a broad menu of export goods categories. For instance, Food and Live Animals, Beverages and Tobacco, Crude Oil, etc. You can already stop here, and see the visualization. However, if you'd like to dig deeper, then proceed to step 3.
  3. Select the Level of Disaggregation: Suppose you'd like to dig deeper than the broad menu. For instance, under the category of "Manufactured goods" you'd like to see the quality of "Handbags," or "Footwear." This is where the level of disaggregation is useful. You can select 2-digit, which is a level below (i.e. Manufactured Goods>>Textiles) or 3-digit, which is two levels below (i.e. Manufactured Goods>> Textiles>> Yarn and synthetic fabric).

So let's say, we know that Vietnam is a strong exporter of garments, particularly footwear. This viz can plot Vietnam and highlight its position in the ladder.
